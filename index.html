Turkey's Justice System

Turkey is a Hell for Journalists

For the real ones of course.

Turkey has experienced a failed coup attempt in 2016 and many believe that it was a fake one organised by the government to strengthen its power. The incident was very complicated and a lot of the things are still not clear. Around 96,000 people got arrested after the incident. 231 of them were journalists. Ahmet Altan is just one of them. He's been released twice and got re-arrested a week after he was released. 165 of those journalists are still under arrest, and 167 of their counterparts are wanted as of May 8, 2020.

Currently, there is no free media in Turkey. All media outlets are under control of the government. The government also has a big influence on the justice system which uses against its opponents.

Babies and Women Behind the Bars

According to Advocates of Silenced Turkey, 864 babies are behind the bars with their moms currently. A lot of those women are housemakers, educators, doctors, and academics.








Pregnant Women

Pregnant women are jailed in Turkey and have been ill-treated. One of them has died due to the negligence of the authorities. Police have also detained many women only hours after they gave birth to their babies.

Human Right Activists

Osman Kavala is one of the well-known human right activists that have been arrested after the coup attempt. He's been aquitted twice but re-arrested and still in jail despite the fact that there is no sufficient evidence that shows he has committed a crime. The State dosn't provide any explanation as to why he is not being released.

Educators

Gokhan Acikkollu is just of the educators that have falsely but intentionally been arrested after the unfortunate coup attempt. He was tortured to death under police custody. Around a year and a half after his death, the Government issued a statement which cleared him of all charges and re-instated him to his teaching position. No kidding!

Covid-19 - Murderers Released, Babies Not

Murderers, rapers, and even mafia leaders have been released as a safety measure to the Covid-19 but babies, women, teacher, doctors, human rights defenders have still been kept in jail. On top of that, 35-40 detainees have been kept in a room for 7. Therefore, the number of the new cases are growing rapidly in the jails. Amnesty International has launched a petition to help release the volnurable unfairly imprisoned people.

Enes Kanter

The NBA Player has recently launched a petition named You're My Hope! aiming to get 1 million signatures to help release the political detainees in Turkey.
